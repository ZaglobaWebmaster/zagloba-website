A History of Tradition

About The Club

Founded in the late 90s, Zagłoba Polish Club was founded on the basis of upholding Polish tradition, culture, and language among University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign students. The original founders recognized the importance of preserving the Polish identity in the setting of higher learning institutions, and Zagłoba was born. Since the early years of the organization, the membership of the organization (average 200 students each year) participate in various cultural, social, and philanthropy events. From Nalesniki Night and Wigilia to Smigus Dyngus on the Quad and folk dancing, students embrace the beautiful aspects of Polish culture. For social events, students attend the Zagłoba Charity Barn Dance, founded in 2002, where proceeds go towards Polish institutions in Chicago and Poland. The club’s main emphasis on charity led to donations to the Polish American Association, Dar Serca, foster homes in Poland, and many other organizations and causes.

Being a member of Zagłoba means being part of a family of scholarly, passionate, and self-motivated individuals that value service and community. Every year, the executive boards and the members seek to expand on creating experiences that encapsulate Polish culture and community for native Polish individuals, Polish-Americans, and those interested in learning about the country.

About The Name

Jan Onufry Zagłoba is a fictional character appearing in several novels of one of Poland's most internationally famous authors, Henryk Sienkiewicz. Jan Zagłoba is first introduced as man living a meaningless life of a lesser noble, trying to survive by exploiting the good faith of others. However, after meeting another character in The Trilogy, Jan Skrzetuski, he becomes drawn into the company of hero-like personas and slowly grows to become worthy of their trust and friendship.

Zagłoba eventually becomes a widely known and respected person and is seen as a symbol of undying optimism and hope. He is regarded as being one of Sienkiewicz's most significant characters and continues to have an enduring influence on Polish culture to this day. To us however, it is his iconic transformation as well as his characteristically cheerful disposition which make him the embodiment of what we strive to offer as an organization.
